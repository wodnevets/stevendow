Client

Vodafone

Role in Project

Lead Visual Designer

Deliverables

UI design, Wireframes, Design system, iconography

Goal

Redesign the UI of Vodafone’s brand new XONE beta product drivexone.

Overview

We undertook a complete redesign of the native iOS and Android app. We updated the UI, created a new IA, and designed a fun and playful experience by implementing gamification.

Result

I Art Directed the badge designs which was part of the gamification. Vodafone global branding design team liked the art direction and used it as a foundation for future Vodafone global branding.

Sketching

You will be able to recognise me by my big yellow ZAP BOOK. This is my trusted old friend and I don't leave the house without it. I always start sketching my ideas on paper, it's faster and easier to find the right look and feel. I start with thumbnails to find the right flow and layout. And from there on I sketch everything on A3 paper.

Prototyping

After finalizing the flow and layout, I create an interactive prototype. I use different software for this process depending on what I'm designing for. In this case, I used an iPhone app called POP (prototype on paper).

Icon & badge designs

I was in charge of the Art Direction and came up with the style for the badges and icons.

Measurements & assets

15 pages specifications document, 300+ assets, 20+ icons, and 40+ unique badges were created.

The results

A fun and easy to use Andriod & iOS app MVP. This led to an adoption of more than 10.000 users in a couple of months.

Steven jumped into our project as visual designer on short notice and delivered with vast speed and professionalism the smartphone app designs for Vodafone’s brand new XONE beta product drivexone. He did the art direction of the iPhone and Android apps and guided three designers. Steven did an excellent job and became an essential part of the agile development team.
