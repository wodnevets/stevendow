About the project

SRXP is an online expense reporting and processing solution. In combination with an online platform the mobile app is the perfect tool for employees to report their expenses. SRXP lowers transaction and processing costs, saves time and space, is easy to manage, is tax technical correct, integrates with financial software and is worldwide accessible. Receipts and expense reports are stored on secure servers. With the mobile app people can process receipts from every location.

My role

I was responsible for gathering the project’s requirements, doing competitive research and analysis of similar services, designing the architecture of the iOS app, wire framing, prototyping and creating final documentation for development.

The challenge

I was asked to help further develop their existing iOS app. They asked me to create a user-friendly, reliable, secure and professional iOS app.

Research & Competitive Analysis

There were similar services out there such as Concur, Declaree, Mobile Expense and Expenditure. I signed up for every service to research their user flows and to understand their business model and see how they compared to SRXP’s concept.

Tools used

  • Paper and pen
  • Photoshop
  • POP
  • Indesign
  • Illustrator

Sketching

I started sketching and paper prototyping. One of the main focusses was to create a seamless registration process. The other thing was to create an app with as many native functionalities as possible to speed up the development process.

For paper prototyping I like to use an app called POP. It's a greate way to create a quick clickable prototype to see if your initial ideas work.

Wireframes & Prototypes

I was initially given some rough wireframes by the stakeholders to demonstrate the basic flow of the app and it was my responsibility to optimize the user experience.

After walking the stakeholders through the paper prototypes and making changes. I started creating wireframes for a high fidelity prototype.

After finetuning and getting the flow right. I like to create hifidelity prototypes with real content and data. By doing this you're able to finetune the flow even further and find dead ends that can be resolved before you start developing.

Documentation

I created a document for the team detailing features first release, Information architecture, Messages triggers and locations, Key screens, user flows and target platform information.

I find it very importsant to go into details on how a product should function and flow. This speeds up the process, less mistake are made and expectations are met during development.

The results

A well thought through, easy to use app with happy customers. This led to a 30% growth in user retention.
